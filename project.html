Sundial

From wikipedial, the free encyclopedia

A sundial measures time by the positon of the sun. The most commonly seen designs, such as the 'ordinary' or standard garden sundial. cast a shadow on flat surface marked with the hours of the day. As the position of the sun changes, the time indicated by shadow changes. However, sundias can be designed for any surface where a fixed object last a predictable shadow. Most sundial design indicate apperent solar time. Minor designed varriations can measures standard and daylight saving time , as well.

History

Sundials in the form of obelisks (3500 BC) and shadow clocks (1500 BC) are known from accient Eygyt, and were developed further by other cultures, including the Chinese, Greek and Roman cultures. A type of sundial without gromon is described in the old old Testament ( Isaih 38:2) The mathematical and astronomer Theodosius of Bithynia (Ca.160 BC - Ca 100 BC) is said to have invented a suniversal sundial that could be used anywhere on Earth. The French astronomer Oronce Fine constructed a sundial of ivory in 1524. The Italian astronomer Giovanni Padovain published a treaties on the sundialin 1570, in which he included instruction for the manufacture and laying out mural (vertical) and horizontal sundial. Giuseppe Biancanis construction instrument ad horologia solaria discuses how to make a perfect sundial, with a accompanying illustrations.

Installation of standard sundials

Many ornamental sundials are designed to be at 45 degrees north. By tilding such a sundial, it may be installed so that it will keep time. However some mass-produced garden sundials are inacurrate because of poor design and can not be correced. A sundial designed for one latitude can be adjusted for use at another latitude by tilting it base so that it style or gromon is parallel to the Earth's axis of rotation so that it points at the north celestil pole in the northern hemisphere, or the south celestial pole in the southern hemisphere. A local standard time zone is normaly 15 degrees wide, but may be modified tto follow geographic and political boundries. A sundial can be rotated around its style or gromon ( which must remain pointed at the celestial pole) to adjust to the local time zone in most cases a rotation in the 7.5 degrees east to 23 degrees west suffice. To correct for daylight savings time, a face needs two sets of numerals or a correction table An information standard is to have numerals in hot color for summer, and in cool color for winter. rotation the sundial will not work wwell because most sundials do not have equal hour angles.
